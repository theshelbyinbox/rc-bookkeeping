## Quick Notes for Shelby

**Why this approach works (sales psychology breakdown):**

- **Removes price anchoring** -- When people see a specific number on a website, they anchor to it. If it feels too high, they leave. By leading with "let's talk," Rachel controls the framing.
- **Creates curiosity, not sticker shock** -- The calculator gives them *enough* information to feel informed, but the copy positions the real answer as something they get through a conversation. This is the same psychology car dealerships use with "call for pricing."
- **Lowers perceived risk** -- Phrases like "no pressure," "nothing more than you need," and "the easiest phone call" all reduce the emotional cost of reaching out.
- **Positions Rachel as the expert** -- Instead of the client self-diagnosing what they need from a checklist, the copy implies that Rachel will help them figure it out. This builds trust and makes her the guide, not a vending machine.
- **The calculator still does its job** -- It shows Rachel is transparent and organized. Even if someone never calls, the calculator builds credibility. But the copy makes sure it's positioned as step one, not the final answer.
